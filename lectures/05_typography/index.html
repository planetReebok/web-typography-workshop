Looking at black & white shapes

Web Typography Workshop @ Letterform Archive
Week 2

Shapes

When typesetting, we have two components to consider. As a typographer, we consider various relationships between them.

  • The black shapes on the page
  • The white shapes on the page

The Overall Website

Consider the pace of dark & light across an entire document or website; how this may signify hierarchy and structure.

  • Black shapes: inversed pages, dense pages
  • White shapes: normal pages, light pages

The Page

  • Black shapes: blocks of paragraphs, images
  • White shapes: page margins, space between columns, paragraphs, images, all components
  • Relevant general CSS: width, margin, padding
  • Relevant CSS Grid: grid-template-columns, grid-gap, grid-column, grid-row

The Paragraph

  • Black shapes: lines of text
  • White shapes: leading (line-height), heading margins / padding, text indents
  • Relevant CSS: line-height, text-align, text-indent, margin, padding.
  • For alternate text directions: writing-mode: vertical-rl;,text-orientation: upright / sideways;
